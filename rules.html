Basics

Team Rules

These rules keep Premier prep simple and fair. The site exists to reduce confusion, not to create drama.

1
Set your availability clearly.Use the Status page before Premier so the team knows who is ready and who is not available.
2
Keep notes useful.Notes should help with practice, maps, tactics or personal prep. Do not store sensitive private information here.
3
Respect admin edits.Admins can update score, status resets, challenges and penalties to keep the team board accurate.
4
No fake data.Do not intentionally change your account, status or notes in a way that misleads the team.
5
Use shared features responsibly.Suggestions, votes, comments and uploaded images are visible to the team where those features are used. Do not use them to spam, impersonate or harass anyone.
Premier

Status Rule

If you do not set either Ready or Not ready by the day before Premier starts, you agree to donate 25 PLN or more to any charity fundraiser. By using this site and playing in the team, you accept this team rule.

Changing your mind is fine. The important part is making the status visible early enough for the team to plan.

Match data

Skill Rating

Skill Rating is an internal progress system based on submitted match reports. Everyone starts at 0 SR. SR can go up after wins, go down after losses and can never drop below 0.

The system uses one main title instead of many rank names. At 10 000 SR a player unlocks the Eteris Player mark. After that, SR keeps rising so the leaderboard can still show who is leading.

Reports may include match result, Average Combat Score, kills, deaths, assists, kill difference, K/D, damage differential, ADR, HS%, KAST, first kills, first deaths and multi-kills. The SR algorithm uses curved weighting, so normal matches stay reasonable while exceptional games or very poor games can move SR more strongly. There is no fixed maximum gain or loss, but match result controls direction: a win can only be 0 or positive SR, and a loss can only be 0 or negative SR.

If OCR is used from a screenshot, the screenshot should be clear and should show one player row from Tracker or the game. Players must correct unread or incorrect values before confirming the report.

Admins may apply a per-player SR adjustment, such as a gain multiplier, loss multiplier or flat correction, when there is a documented reason. This does not rewrite the base algorithm; it changes how that player's final SR change is scaled.

Screenshots used for local OCR should not be stored in Supabase by default. The site should store only the extracted numbers, calculated SR change and review status. If proof screenshots are ever added for review, they should be temporary and removed when no longer needed.

Some accounts may require admin review before the rating change is applied. Reviewed reports can be approved or rejected by admins. Pending reports store full submitted stats only until approval or rejection; after that, only a short rating log remains: player, date, decision and rating change.

The Eteris Player target may be used for rewards. The best player below Eteris Player may also be rewarded, so both reaching the title and chasing it honestly matter.

Ignoring the duty to submit required match data, repeatedly leaving reports missing, or intentionally submitting incorrect data may result in a charity penalty or a team-prize penalty decided by admins.

Charity

Penalties

Penalties are internal team accountability rules. They are not payments to Eteris. When a penalty is assigned, the player should donate the amount to a charity fundraiser of their choice unless the team agrees otherwise.

The public penalty list is only meant to show who still has an amount to handle. It should not be used to shame people.

Development

Suggestions

Suggestions exist to improve the team site. Write a clear title, explain the problem and describe the proposed solution. Voting helps show interest, but does not guarantee implementation.

The author may edit a suggestion once and may permanently delete their own suggestion. Deleting a suggestion also removes its votes and comments. Admins can change statuses, add a team response and remove suggestions when moderation is needed.

Keep discussion useful. Repeated ideas, joke spam, personal attacks and intentionally misleading reports may be removed.

Data

Privacy & Data

The site stores only the data needed for the team dashboard: account name, login code, display name, avatar link or uploaded avatar, notes, suggestions, suggestion votes and comments, Premier status, challenges, skill rating, temporary pending match reports, short rating logs and charity penalty amounts.

Private notes are intended for your own use inside the team app. Site admins and database maintainers may still be able to access stored data while maintaining the project, so do not store passwords, personal documents or sensitive information.

You can use the Account page to change your display name, avatar and login code. You can also delete your personal notes, display name and avatar from the Account page. Authors can delete their own suggestions. Admins and database maintainers may remove shared content when required.

Uploaded avatars use shared Supabase storage. When uploaded files are replaced or deleted, the site attempts to remove files that are no longer used by any account. Direct external image links remain controlled by their original host.

Conduct

Ethics & Fair Play

Eteris is built around fair play. Do not use the site to share cheats, stolen accounts, private data, harassment, doxxing or anything that breaks Riot Games rules.

Feedback should be direct, but not toxic. The point of tracking status, challenges and notes is to help people improve and keep Premier organized.
